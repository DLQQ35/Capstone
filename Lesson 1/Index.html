Some Cubes


Cubing Today

Cubing is a technique that encourages deeper thinking and creative expression by examining a topic from multiple angles, much like looking at a cube from different sides. It's a helpful tool for various subjects, including writing, math, and science, and can be adapted for different age groups and learning styles.

The Rubik's Cube is still very popular today! It's enjoyed by people of all ages around the world, both casually and competitively. There's a whole community dedicated to speedcubing, where people practice solving the cube as fast as possible, and there are even official competitions and world records.

Beyond speedcubing, the Rubik's Cube has also maintained its status as a cultural icon and a fascinating puzzle. It's often used as a tool for teaching concepts in mathematics and computer science, like group theory and algorithms. There are also many variations of the original 3x3 cube, including larger sizes (4x4, 5x5, etc.), different shapes (Pyraminx, Megaminx), and electronic versions. Artists and designers also continue to find creative ways to incorporate the Rubik's Cube into their work.

About WCA

WCA stands for the World Cube Association. It's the international governing body for speedcubing competitions. The WCA organizes and regulates official competitions for solving the Rubik's Cube and other related puzzles as quickly as possible. They set the rules, maintain world rankings, and certify official results. If you hear about speedcubing world records or major competitions, they are almost always sanctioned by the WCA.

WCA also provides resources and support for speedcubing enthusiasts, including forums, events, and educational materials. They also organize international competitions and provide prizes for top performers. If you're interested in speedcubing, the WCA is a great place to start.
